Peekaboo WhatsApp
Chat Analysis

646 conversations reviewed  ·  81 analyzed in depth  ·  10 conversions identified
Executive Summary
646 conversations were reviewed. 517 (80%) never received a consumer reply after the Peekaboo greeting — the largest single loss point in the funnel. Among the 81 relevant or mixed conversations analyzed in depth, 10 showed a conversion signal, yielding a 12.3% conversion rate on the engaged set. Conversion took an average of 100.4 hours and roughly 3 nudges. The clearest mid-funnel blockers were silence after initial interest (29 conversations), price sensitivity (24), and profile availability / fit issues (12 + 12). The biggest lever is improving first-message reply rates, followed by earlier qualification and clearer pricing communication.

Key Patterns
80.0% drop before any customer reply
517 of 646 conversations unanswered after the Peekaboo greeting — only 20% moved to two-way conversation.
→ Largest funnel leak; downstream improvements have limited impact until this is addressed.
12.3% conversion rate in engaged relevant chats
10 of 81 relevant/mixed conversations converted: 4 confirmed paid, 6 application-started.
→ The sales flow can convert when prospects engage; too few engaged chats reach a committed next step.
45.7% asked for Filipino / Filipina maids
37 of 81 conversations specified Filipino/Filipina, far ahead of African (11) and Indian (7).
→ Shortages in Filipina profiles likely hurt conversions more than any other single supply gap.
35.8% of non-conversions ended in silence
29 conversations were coded as went_silent — the largest named drop-off reason among non-converted chats.
→ Deals aren't being lost through explicit rejection; follow-up quality is the main lever.
29.6% showed price sensitivity
24 of 81 conversations marked price-sensitive; 8 explicitly asked for discounts.
→ Price framing and value explanation need to happen early before prospects hesitate.
4.2-day average conversion window
Average time from Peekaboo outreach to conversion was 100.4 hours with 3 nudges.
→ A planned multi-day follow-up flow is necessary; conversions rarely happen on first contact.
4.9% showed direct skepticism about maids.cc (low confidence)
4 of 81 conversations showed explicit skepticism about maids.cc; none about Peekaboo itself.
→ Trust concerns attach to service handling, contracts, or payments — sample is small.

Proposed Experiments
Experiment 1 — First Message
If maids.cc replaces the current first outreach with a one-question quick-reply opener, more Peekaboo leads will reply.
A/B test current greeting vs. shorter version asking "Are you looking for live-in or live-out?" — delay pricing until after the first reply.
✦ Metric: Reply rate within 24 hours & qualified-conversation rate
Experiment 2 — Profile Fit Check
If profiles are shown only after a structured fit check (nationality, living arrangement, emirate, childcare), fewer prospects will drop due to mismatch.
Pilot structured intake for half of new engaged leads before any profile sharing; compare mismatch complaints with current flow.
✦ Metric: Unavailable-profile complaints & profile-to-application rate
Experiment 3 — Pricing Explainer
If agents send a one-screen pricing breakdown before deeper price discussion, fewer prospects will object on cost.
For half of chats that ask about cost, send explainer immediately after qualification; compare outcomes against normal handling.
✦ Metric: Price-sensitive chats that continue after pricing & application-start rate
Experiment 4 — Silent Lead Recovery
If silent prospects receive a personalized recovery message tied to their last stated preference, some stalled leads will re-engage.
For two weeks, send personalized 24-hour follow-ups to all newly silent leads; compare reactivation against a control using the standard reminder.
✦ Metric: Reactivation rate & conversion rate among previously silent prospects
Experiment 5 — Post-Payment Workflow
If automated document reminders stop once documents are received and exceptions get a human owner, paid-customer frustration will decline.
Pilot on all paid/application-started cases for two weeks; track duplicate reminder complaints vs. current process.
✦ Metric: Repeated document-request complaints, support touches per paid case, refund-risk complaints
High-Confidence Findings
  • ●517 of 646 conversations (80%) unanswered — first-message engagement is the biggest funnel loss.
  • ●10 of 81 relevant conversations converted — 12.3% conversion rate in engaged set.
  • ●37 of 81 (45.7%) asked for Filipino/Filipina maids — dominant nationality preference.
  • ●29 conversations went silent — largest named drop-off reason among non-converted chats.
  • ●24 of 81 (29.6%) were price-sensitive; 8 asked explicitly for discounts.
  • ●42 of 81 conversations stated live-in or live-out preferences — core qualification variable.
  • ●37 of 81 explicitly mentioned childcare needs — demand is childcare-led, not generic housekeeping.
  • ●12 + 12 conversations hit profile shortage or unavailability — repeated supply-fit friction.
  • ●Converted chats averaged 100.4 hours and 3 nudges — multi-day follow-up is usually required.
Low-Confidence Signals & Caveats
  • Trust issues appear aimed more at maids.cc handling than at Peekaboo, but based on only 4 conversations.
  • Muslim religion preference appeared in only 2 conversations — too small to drive workflow changes.
  • Arabic-language support may help but only 2 conversations explicitly requested it.
  • Marital-status preference appeared only 3 times explicitly — should not shape standard messaging yet.
Data Caveats
  • Conversion is inferred from chat text only — payment may not have been fully verified.
  • Preference data reflects only prospects who explicitly stated preferences — silent prospects are not counted.
