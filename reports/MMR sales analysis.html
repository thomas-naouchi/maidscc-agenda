Data Quality Resolution
✔ Peekaboo time‑to‑close is now measurable
The initial dataset used LAST_STATUS_CHANGE_DATE for Peekaboo, producing negative durations in 11 of 13 cases. The corrected extract adds RESOLUTION_DATE, which captures the actual trial‑to‑complete transition. Per the agreed rule, this report now computes time‑to‑close as:

  • Malayan (V2V): creation → LAST_STATUS_CHANGE_DATE
  • Peekaboo (Completed): creation → RESOLUTION_DATE
  • Peekaboo (Trial): excluded (not yet resolved)

With this fix, all 11 Peekaboo Completed rows yield positive durations. However, 6 of those 11 were logged retroactively (creation ≈ resolution within seconds), so only 5 reflect genuine funnel time. All statistics below use only clean, positive‑duration rows.
Executive Summary
In April and May 2026, MMR closed 34 sales: 21 Malayan (V2V) and 13 Peekaboo (11 completed, 2 still in trial). Filipinas represent 73.5% of all sales (25 of 34). The channel mix shifted dramatically — Peekaboo went from 17% of April completions to 63% in May (partial month), making it the primary growth channel.

Time‑to‑close benchmarks based on 23 clean records (18 Malayan + 5 real‑time Peekaboo):
  • Malayan: median 5.1 d, mean 7.5 d, P80 11.5 d
  • Peekaboo (real‑time only): median 7.8 d, mean 6.2 d, P80 8.8 d
  • Combined department: median 6.2 d, mean 7.2 d, P80 10.7 d

Two workflow patterns limit metric coverage: 6 of 11 Peekaboo cases were logged after the fact (creation = resolution), and 3 Malayan rows on 19 May appear bulk‑backdated. Together, 9 of 34 sales (26%) are excluded from duration analysis. Addressing real‑time complaint creation would recover these rows.

Observed Patterns
Pattern 1 — Peekaboo time‑to‑close is measurable and slightly slower but more predictable (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Real‑time Peekaboo median 7.8 d vs Malayan 5.1 d. However Peekaboo P80 is 8.8 d (vs 11.5 d) and max is 9.9 d (vs 34 d). Peekaboo has a tighter, more reliable distribution.
Implication: Peekaboo trades a marginally slower median for far less variance — a good channel for SLAs once sample size grows.
Pattern 2 — 55% of Peekaboo Completed cases were logged retroactively (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
6 of 11 cases have creation ≈ resolution (≤ 10 sec). The complaint record was created after the sale was already complete, so the funnel time is invisible.
Implication: Real‑time complaint creation (at trial start) would recover SLA coverage from 45% to 100%.
Pattern 3 — Three Malayan sales bulk‑backdated on 19 May (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
IDs 694048, 694050, 694059 created within 7 minutes; all have last‑status‑change before creation. Looks like retroactive batch logging.
Implication: 26% of the dataset (9 of 34) is affected by post‑hoc complaint creation. This biases the reported time‑to‑close toward sales captured in real time.
Pattern 4 — Channel mix flipped April→May
April 83% Malayan; May (partial) 38% Malayan. Peekaboo completions already surpass April volume.
Implication: Peekaboo is now the primary growth channel. Operational tooling and real‑time logging must match this shift.
Pattern 5 — Malayan time‑to‑close has a long tail
3 of 18 Malayan sales took >14 days; max 34 days. Mean is inflated (7.5 d) while median (5.1 d) and P80 (11.5 d) reflect typical performance.
Implication: P80 = 12 days is the most defensible internal SLA for Malayan.
Pattern 6 — Filipina concentration
73.5% of all sales are Filipina, even higher on Peekaboo (9 of 13). Filipina supply converts at a disproportionately high rate.
Implication: MMR volume is exposed to Filipina supply. Diversification efforts should be monitored closely.
Pattern 7 — Possible batch‑creation workflow (LOW CONFIDENCE)
Several retroactive Peekaboo records appear in close time pairs. May reflect agents processing multiple maids at shift start.
Implication: A quick ops check can confirm whether batch creation is routine, which would help explain the retroactive logging.
Recommendations
1 — Workflow
Create Peekaboo complaint records at trial start, not at completion. This turns all retroactive rows into measurable funnel entries.
Operational rule: log a complaint within 24 h of a lead entering the 1000 AED trial tier.
✦ Peekaboo SLA coverage rises from 5/11 (45%) toward 11/11 (100%).
2 — SLA
Adopt P80 = 11 days as the department‑wide soft SLA (4 out of 5 sales close ≤ 11 days).
Track monthly P80 by channel. Flag cases approaching 11 days for supervisor review.
✦ Metric: % sales closed ≤ 11 d; target ≥ 80%.
3 — Audit
Investigate the 19 May Malayan batch (IDs 694048/50/59) to confirm whether retroactive logging is a recurring norm.
A short conversation with the agent who created them will reveal if this is standard practice or a one‑off cleanup.
✦ Outcome: either confirmed data quality or a documented caveat.
4 — Strategic
Monitor the Malayan→Peekaboo mix. If Peekaboo sustains >70% of completions for two months, rebalance agent focus and tooling.
Already at 63% in a partial May; June/July will confirm if this is structural.
✦ Monthly Peekaboo share of completed sales.
5 — Schema
Add a PEEKABOO_TRIAL_START_DATE field. Together with RESOLUTION_DATE, this would create a two‑stage funnel: trial start → completion.
One‑time schema change; enables trial duration analysis and early warning for stalled trials.
✦ Outcome: full pipeline visibility instead of a single creation‑to‑resolution window.

Caveats & Limitations
  • Only 34 sales — breakdowns by nationality/month are indicative, not authoritative.
  • May data covers 1–20 May only; April vs May volume comparisons are unfair.
  • Peekaboo clean cohort = 5 rows; re‑evaluate with 2–3 more months of data.
  • Retroactive logging (9 of 34 rows) means reported TTC metrics reflect a non‑random subset of all sales.
  • No revenue, agent ID, or trial‑start date in this extract — financial/attribution analysis not possible.
  • Two Peekaboo Trial‑only cases may simply need more time; not a structural failure signal yet.
